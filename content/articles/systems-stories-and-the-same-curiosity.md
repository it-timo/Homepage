Software, music, and worldbuilding can look like unrelated bodies of work when they are arranged by medium. The connection becomes clearer when they are arranged by question.

How do complex things emerge from simple rules? How does identity persist while a system changes? What does responsibility mean when influence is indirect? How do people and systems retain context, recover from disruption, and reconnect?

DNS Database approaches persistence through storage lifecycles, tests, and documentation. JARVIS approaches it through memory and orchestration. More Powerful Passenger turns emergence and indirect responsibility into a civilization-scale simulation. Grounded Fire explores reconnection after emotional and technological distance.

The mediums are different because the questions need different forms. The archive exists to preserve those connections rather than flattening the work into one category.
