Used To Be Easy centers on the Walker: a 36-year-old man with dark hair, light stubble, and an emotionally restrained presence. The character carries memory through observation rather than performance.

The warm silhouettes around him are memories of human connection. They are not ghosts. That distinction matters because the visual idea is about distance from a lived relationship, not a supernatural visitation.

Warmth belongs to remembered connection; restraint belongs to the present. The contrast gives nostalgia a physical language without making it sentimental or visually loud.
