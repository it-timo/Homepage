Inspection can reveal a defect. It cannot retroactively make a system understandable.

Quality is designed through explicit boundaries, useful feedback, testable behavior, and documentation that preserves why the system works the way it does. Testing remains essential, but it is strongest when the architecture has made reliable behavior possible rather than accidental.
